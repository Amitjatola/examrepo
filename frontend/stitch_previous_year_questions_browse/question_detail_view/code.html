A particle of mass m moves in a circular orbit under the influence of a central potential field V(r) = -k/r, where k is a positive constant. If the Bohr quantization condition is applied, what is the expression for the total energy of the particle in the n-th orbit?

lightbulb

check_circle Correct Answer: B

The centripetal force is provided by the central potential force. Given V(r) = -k/r, the force F(r) is given by the negative gradient of the potential:

F(r) = -dV/dr = -d/dr(-k/r) = -k/r²

For a circular orbit, the magnitude of this force provides the centripetal acceleration: mv²/r = k/r² which implies mv² = k/r.

Using Bohr's quantization condition L = mvr = nħ, we can substitute v = nħ / (mr) into the force equation to solve for the radius r and subsequently the total energy.

Source: JEE Advanced 2023 Paper 1, Physics Section.
